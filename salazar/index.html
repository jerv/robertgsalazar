History

Dr. Salazar was born and raised in Corcoran California. He is one of eight children born into a Spanish-speaking household with farm worker roots. He learned the value of hard work at early age. After completing his training (which included a fellowship in interventional pain) he joined the faculty at U.C.S.F. for four years as the clinic director of an embryonic pain clinic. Dr. Salazar spent the next eight years in Tucson Arizona administering operating room anesthesia and involved in two out-patient pain centers. Sister Ruth Marie Nickerson, (CEO of Saint Agnes Hospital) invited Dr. Salazar to return to his home in the San Joaquin Valley in 1993 to start a pain clinic affiliated with Saint Agnes.

In 2000 Robert Salazar, M.D., Brian Clague, M.D., Berj Kalamkarian, M.D., and Fred Williams, M.D., joined forces and built a 12,000 square foot building dedicated to pain management. It included a 4,000 square foot state of the art two room outpatient surgical center. In 2011 Dr. Salazar acquired Dr. Kalamkarian’s pain practice. William J. Johncox, D.C., P.A.-C joined Dr. Salazar in 2011 along with the acquisition.

Dr. Salazar is currently the Medical Director of Comprehensive Pain Management Center. He is committed in helping patients improve the quality of their daily activities by integrating a multi-disciplinary approach to pain management to include pharmacological management, intervention including principled experience in neuromodulation therapies. Dr. Salazar enjoys spending time with his family, traveling and classic Western movies.
